# B2: Writing Real Specifications

A good spec is a contract for what to build.

<div class="bad-practice">
<strong>Bad Spec</strong><br>
"Build login with email and password."
</div>

<div class="best-practice">
<strong>Good Spec Structure</strong>
<ul>
<li>Objective</li>
<li>Requirements with inputs and outputs</li>
<li>Error scenarios</li>
<li>Acceptance criteria</li>
<li>Constraints and out-of-scope items</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="exercise">
<strong>Try it now</strong>
Rewrite your B1 shopping-cart idea as one feature spec:
<ul>
<li>Objective</li>
<li>At least 3 requirements</li>
<li>At least 2 acceptance criteria</li>
<li>At least 1 error scenario</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
